# India's Youth Unemployment Crisis — A Data Story

## 📌 About This Project
A data visualization dashboard exploring 30+ years of India's youth unemployment, 
GDP growth, inflation, and rupee depreciation — and how it connects to the viral 
CJP (Cockroach Janta Party) movement of May 2026.

## 🔍 Key Findings
- 44.5% of Indian graduates aged 20–24 are unemployed
- Youth make up 83% of India's total unemployed population
- India's GDP grew 7x since 2000 — but youth jobs never caught up
- The rupee lost half its value — ₹45 to ₹84 per dollar since 2000
- Urban women face the worst unemployment at 9% — nearly 1 in 10
- Haryana (37.4%) and Bihar (32.1%) lead state-wise youth unemployment

## 📊 Dashboard Pages
**Page 1 — The Big Picture**
- 30-year youth unemployment trend

- India state map colored by unemployment intensity

**Page 2 — Cost of Living Crisis**
- Rupee depreciation timeline (2000–2026)
- Gender & urban/rural unemployment breakdown

## 🗂️ Data Sources
| Dataset | Source |
|---------|--------|

| Monthly Unemployment | PLFS / MoSPI India |
| GDP Growth | World Bank |
| Inflation (CPI) | World Bank |
| USD/INR Exchange Rate | RBI / Trading Economics |
| State-wise Unemployment | PLFS State Reports |
